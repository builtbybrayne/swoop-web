Four regions, four different Patagonias.

Patagonia is bigger and more varied than most first-time visitors realise. Where you go shapes everything — terrain, weather, logistics, company. Start with a region that matches what you're after.

Torres del Paine

Chile's trekking icon — the W, the O circuit, and the three towers. Busiest in Jan/Feb; shoulder-season has space and weather windows.

See the W Trek →

El Chaltén

Day-hike heaven across the border in Argentina. Fitz Roy and Cerro Torre on clear mornings, village life in between.

Ask a specialist

Carretera Austral

A slow road between fjords, temperate rainforest, and hanging glaciers. Self-drive or small-group overland.

Ask a specialist

Tierra del Fuego

The end of the road. Ushuaia, Beagle Channel wildlife, and the gateway to Antarctica cruises.

Ask a specialist

When to go

Patagonia's main season runs roughly November through March. Each region has its own rhythms — specialists can help you sort out windows without overcommitting. Or just ask.
